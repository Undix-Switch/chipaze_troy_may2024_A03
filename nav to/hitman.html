Hitman



Hitman is a stealth video game franchise created by IO Interactive1. The series revolves around a cloned contract killer named Agent 47, who travels around the world to assassinate various targets assigned to him by the fictional International Contract Agency (ICA)1.



Here are some key aspects of the Hitman game series:

Gameplay:

The gameplay focuses on freedom of approach, with most levels placing the player in a large sandbox location where they have free rein to explore and find different ways to reach and eliminate their targets1. Stealth is a major component of the gameplay, and players are given various tools to accomplish their objectives, such as suppressed weaponry, items which can be used as improvised weapons, and the ability to take disguises1.



Series Evolution:

The first game in the franchise, Hitman: Codename 47, was published in 2000 and introduced many of the features which would become staples of the series1. The series has since had a number of new titles, the most recent being Hitman 3, which was released in 20211. The World of Assassination trilogy, which includes Hitman (2016), Hitman 2 (2018), and Hitman 3 (2021), marked a return to the classic Hitman formula, featuring large sandbox levels1.



Reception:

While the series has been praised for its depth and freedom of approach, it has also been criticized for its high difficulty and the necessity of trial and error1.
